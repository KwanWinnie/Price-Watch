What factors affect F&B prices across retailers and product types?

Different types of products, such as high-quality or specialty items, often come with a higher price due to factors like premium ingredients, production methods, and certifications. Branding and reputation also play a role, as recognized and reputable brands may charge a premium for their products. Additionally, packaging and presentation can contribute to pricing, with unique or aesthetically appealing packaging often associated with higher prices. On the retailer side, different types of stores have varying pricing strategies. Specialty stores and gourmet shops typically charge higher prices, while discount stores and supermarkets offer more affordable options. The location of stores, supply chain dynamics, and distribution channels further influence pricing. Ultimately, consumers have the flexibility to compare prices, consider their budget and preferences, and choose the option that best suits their needs.
